Imagine a World without Numbers!

In a lift, if floor numbers are not in order, how will you know whether the lift is going up or down?

Imagine a chocolate broken into pieces and sharing it with your friends. You may give away a few pieces to your friend, but you know... It was one whole chocolate.

When you add chairs for guests, sweets for friends, candles on a cake - silently at the back of your mind you are doing addition.

Without comparison, will it be possible for you to know that the World's talles statue of Lord Ram was unveiled at Gokarn Partagali Math in the year 2025? Would you be able to know who is the fastest runner, the smallest animal?

From helping your mother in the kitchen to sharing snacks with your friends, life full of stories. And these stories turn into memories that stay with you for lifetime.

Ask your child to count chairs, plates or sweets when guests come so they see how addition happens in real life.

When you read news or watch TV together point cut dates, ages, distances are score's so your child notices numbers every where.
